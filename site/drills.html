Morphology drills

"The problem about Latin is that you can study it for six years and still not be able to read a Latin sentence".

Such begins Professor William C. Dowling’s webpage on his unorthodox technique for learning to read Latin fluently: Latin by the Dowling Method. His recipe for success is as follows:

  1. Learn a few simple concepts about Latin grammar, documented on his webpage (1 hour).
  2. Memorize six of the tables at the back of Wheelock’s Latin (6 months).
  3. Work through Hans Ørberg's Lingua Latina, a wonderful workbook on Latin written in Latin (18 months).

These Morphology drills will help you with the second goal: memorizing the tables. On his webpage, Professor Dowling recommends that you write out each table 200 times (but only 100 times for the Adjectives table). Using Morphology drills, you can type the tables instead of writing them out by hand. Moreover, we mark your words in green or red to show which are correct and which are incorrect.

In the Morphology drills the tables of the original method were changed and augmented based on Robert J. Henle's Latin Grammar. This grammar is part of four part Latin course, which from our point of view is even better than widely acclaimed Lingua Latina. Reading Henle's Latin instead of Lingua Latina can be recommended as the third step of Dowling Method.

Also, in the Morphology drills we attempted to compile analogous morphological tables for Attic Greek and Demotic Greek.
